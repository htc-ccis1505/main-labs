Let there be flight!

We're going to fly our dragon around the stage, but we need to make sure that he stays on the stage and doesn't fly away where we can't see him.

Recall from Lab 1: Position on the Stage that the stage has min and max coordinates of -180 and -180, and max and min y coordinates of -240 and 240.

We can use a forever loop with a move [] block to fly the dragon around, but he will soon fly off the stage.


To prevent that, we can alter the direction the dragon sprite will move in using the point in direction [] block. By changing the direction by a value between 90 and 180 we can get the dragon moving back on the stage. By using a random value, we'll make the flight more interesting.

Try it!
Use blocks to conditionally change the direction of the dragon when he starts to wander too close to the edge.
Too close to the edge is about:
- a min x position of -180 or a max x position of 180
- a min y position of -135 and a max y position of 140

If your dragon seems to stick on the edges, adding a change x by: [] or a change y by: [] to bump him in the opposite direction may help.
